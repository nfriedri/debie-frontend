Bias Evaluation Scores


ECT ‒ Embedding Coherence Test

It quantifies the amount of explicit bias BE={T1, T2, A}. Unlike WEAT, it compares vectors of target sets T1 and T2 (averaged over the constituent terms) with vectors from a single attribute set A. ECT first computes the mean vectors for the target sets T1 and T2: µ1 = 1/|T1| * Σ t1∈T1 t1 and µ2 = 1/|T2| * Σ t2∈T2 t2. Next, for both µ1 and µ2 it computes the (cosine) similarities with vectors of all a ∈ A. The two resultant vectors of similarity scores, s1 (for T1) and s2 (for T2) are used to obtain the final ECT score. It is the Spearman’s rank correlation between the rank orders of s1 and s2 ‒ the higher the correlation, the lower the bias.
